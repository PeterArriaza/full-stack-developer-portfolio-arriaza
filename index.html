About Me

I currently work as a software engineer for the National Board of Medical Examiners, where I maintain backend microservices and batch jobs used in scoring and delivering exam results. I primarily use Java with Spring Boot and use Hibernate/JPA to interface with our Oracle databases. I transitioned to this position in June 2020 after graduating from the University of Pennsylvania in 2016 and working as a mechanical engineer for four years. I completed the Thinkful part time bootcamp for fullstack web development in 2019.

In 2019 I joined Phlask, a Code For Philly project, whose goal is to build a web app that allows people to find free water and food in Philadelphia. We hope that through the app, people are empowered to ask (hence phl-ask) for things that we feel everyone should have access to. On the team I am a lead front end React developer and build out features that we have collaboratively identified as priority issues. We use Slack to communicate, Github for version control and issue tracking, and Figma for design work. Our app uses Google Firebase to house our pin locations and is deployed through a Docker container that is hosted on AWS.

In my free time, I play ultimate frisbee and practice yoga. Quarantine has lead to a revival in my golf game and I'm now a 15 handicap. I'm also a volunteer tree tender with the Pennsylvania Horitcultural Society.
